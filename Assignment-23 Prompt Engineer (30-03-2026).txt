## **Objective**

To understand the importance of prompt engineering by comparing weak and strong prompts across different use cases such as resume writing, business ideas, and study planning.

---

## **Introduction**

Prompt engineering is the process of designing effective inputs to get accurate and useful outputs from AI systems. A well-structured prompt provides clarity, context, and specific instructions, while a weak prompt leads to vague or incomplete responses.

---

## **1. Resume Writing**

### ❌ Weak Prompt:

> Write a resume.

### ✅ Strong Prompt:

> Create a professional resume for a computer science student with skills in Python, Java, and web development. Include sections for education, skills, projects, and achievements. Keep it concise and formatted.

### 🔍 Comparison:

* Weak prompt lacks detail → produces generic output
* Strong prompt gives structure, skills, and purpose → produces tailored resume

---

## **2. Business Idea**

### ❌ Weak Prompt:

> Give me a business idea.

### ✅ Strong Prompt:

> Suggest a unique and low-cost business idea for a college student interested in technology and sustainability. Include target audience, startup cost, and potential profit.

### 🔍 Comparison:

* Weak prompt → very broad and unfocused
* Strong prompt → specific, practical, and actionable

---

## **3. Study Plan**

### ❌ Weak Prompt:

> Make a study plan.

### ✅ Strong Prompt:

> Create a 2-week study plan for a student preparing for exams in Mathematics and Science. Include daily topics, revision time, and short breaks.

### 🔍 Comparison:

* Weak prompt → unclear duration and subjects
* Strong prompt → structured and time-bound plan

---

## **4. General Writing Task**

### ❌ Weak Prompt:

> Write something about AI.

### ✅ Strong Prompt:

> Write a 150-word paragraph explaining the advantages and disadvantages of Artificial Intelligence in education.

### 🔍 Comparison:

* Weak prompt → vague topic
* Strong prompt → clear length, topic, and direction

---

## **Key Differences Between Weak and Strong Prompts**

| Feature        | Weak Prompt | Strong Prompt        |
| -------------- | ----------- | -------------------- |
| Clarity        | Low         | High                 |
| Detail         | Minimal     | Specific             |
| Output Quality | Generic     | Focused and relevant |
| Structure      | Missing     | Clearly defined      |
